Three Findings That Matter

The study produced three conclusions with direct implications for how BFSI fund performance should be evaluated.

FINDING 01
The Diversification Paradox

ABSL (35 stocks) beat NIMF (27 stocks) on every consensus holding: HDFC Bank, Insurance, and MFI. NIMF still won by 4.18% overall. The alpha came entirely from the non-headline 75% of the portfolio, positions 11 to 30, not the fund factsheet. Top-10 analysis is misleading.

FINDING 02
Regime Determines the Verdict

80% of the 3-year performance gap crystallised in one 15-month window: the RBI Tightening cycle. NIMF's Sharpe doubled ABSL's in that window (0.827 vs 0.423). Evaluating any fund without isolating market regime produces a verdict that is structurally incomplete.

FINDING 03
Stock Count Is Not a Risk Measure

The 27-stock concentrated fund delivered lower volatility, lower max drawdown, and better downside protection than the 35-stock fund across all three regimes. Sortino ratio and Max Drawdown are the correct risk proxies. Both favoured concentration consistently.

Methodology

1,095 daily NAV observations. 26 quarterly holding snapshots. Regime-specific peak drawdown resets. Cumulative index cross-validated against direct NAV at maximum delta of 0.05%. All formulas documented. All limitations disclosed. Every number independently verifiable from AMFI and NSE source data.

Compliance Recovery Under Regulatory Pressure

KFintech  ·  Operations and Strategy  ·  2024-25

LTCG Calculation Error: Incorrect TDS Deduction for NRI Investors

RTA system generating Long-Term Capital Gains statements showing zero instead of negative figures, overstating LTCG and triggering excess TDS at Central Processing Cell level

10-Day Resolution
The Problem
Incorrect LTCG data flowing from RTA system to TDS Central Processing Cell

Capital gains statements being generated and dispatched to NRI investors contained a critical calculation error: the Long-Term Capital Gains column was showing zero instead of negative figures for investors who had incurred an LTCG loss. This overstated LTCG at the scheme level. Those inflated figures were being passed directly to the TDS Central Processing Cell, resulting in TDS deductions higher than legally required. The error affected investor-facing statements (SOAs), scheme-level capital gain summaries, and downstream regulatory reporting simultaneously.

Step 01: Detection
Root Cause Identification from Investor Escalation

Investor submitted FY 24-25 capital gains statement flagging that line items showed zero where negative LTCG should appear, and that scheme-level capital gain totals were incorrect as a result. Confirmed this as a systemic calculation logic failure across multiple folios, not an isolated data entry error.

Step 02: Scope and Escalation
Multi-Stakeholder Containment

Confirmed the error was not folio-specific. Escalated simultaneously to Operations, AMC client, and internal IT per SOP. Isolated affected folios to map full regulatory exposure before any external escalation occurred.

Step 03: Validation
UAT Environment Verification

Migrated the affected folio to UAT environment post IT logic fix. Verified the updated capital gains statement: negative LTCG values now correctly reflected in the Long-Term Capital Gains column. Scheme-level totals reconciled. Confirmed fix was a logic correction at source, not a data patch.

Step 04: Production Sign-Off
Verified and Approved for Live Deployment

Confirmed UAT output against the original investor-flagged statement. All LTCG loss figures now showing correctly as negative. Formally requested production deployment. Attached verified CGS for affected folio (Quant Multi Cap Fund, Direct Plan, INF966L01614) as documented proof of resolution.
